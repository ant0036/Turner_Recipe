Chicken Thighs

Quick and easy chicken thighs with a creamy mustard pan sauce. Perfect pick for a quick and delicious meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound chicken thighs
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 small shallot, diced
  • 1/3 cup dry white wine
  • 1 tablespoon dijon mustard
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 3 tablespoons freshly chopped parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Pat the chicken thighs dry with a paper towel. Season them with the garlic powder and 1 teaspoon each of salt and pepper.
  2. Add the oil to a large nonstick or cast-iron skillet over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, place the chicken and cook for 6 minutes, then flip and cook for another 4 minutes. Transfer chicken to a plate.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of the butter to the same skillet. Once it's melted, stir in the garlic and shallot. Cook, stirring often, until the shallot softens, about 5 minutes.
  4. Whisk in the mustard and the remaining tablespoon of butter. Whisk in the cream and turn off the heat.
  5. Stir in the parsley and taste the sauce- if needed add another pinch of salt and pepper.

Tips and Tricks

  • The garlic and shallot can be cut up beforehand and stored in the fridge until ready to use.
  • If you aren't sure if the chicken is done, check the internal temperature. If it is done, it should be 165Â°.
